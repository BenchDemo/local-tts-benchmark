01 Speed — real-time factor

Synthesis seconds per second of audio produced; the median across all utterances, each utterance the median of 10 runs. Below 1.0 is faster than real time. GPU runs hot on CUDA, CPU runs cold.

02 Cold start

Seconds from process launch to ready.

03 Memory footprint

Peak resident memory of the model process.

04 Quality — predicted MOS & intelligibility

MOS is the naturalness rating a listener would give (predicted by UTMOS22, 1–5, higher is better); WER is the share of words a Whisper transcription gets wrong versus the source text (lower is better) — how intelligible the speech is. The ledger's MOS min column shows each model's worst single clip: a high average with a low minimum means it occasionally produces a dud.

05 The listening room

Same text, every voice. Card edges follow the device — GPU hot, CPU cold. Cloning-capable models (◈) share one reference voice; the rest use their stock voice. One clip plays at a time.

Glossary

RTF
Real-time factor: seconds of compute per second of audio produced. 0.1 means 10× faster than real time. Lower is better.
MOS
Mean opinion score: predicted listener rating of naturalness (UTMOS22 model), scale 1–5. Higher is better.
WER
Word error rate: fraction of words Whisper mis-hears when transcribing the clip back, compared to the source text. Lower is better.
RSS
Resident set size: peak RAM used by the model process.
